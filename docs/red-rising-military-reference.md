> **Spoilers:** This document contains spoilers through *Light Bringer*.
>
> **Scope:** A consolidated reference covering weapons, armor, warships, military organization, major campaigns from the original trilogy through *Light Bringer*, clawDrills, Drachenjägers, Titans, Storm Gods, and Lovelock engines — plus a battle compendium and named-ship registry for battle-recreation use.
>
> **Canon caveat:** Pierce Brown often gives vivid operational descriptions without a formal technical manual. Hard measurements and explicit mechanisms are labeled as such; where the books leave gaps, the most plausible interpretation is identified as inference.

## Verification changelog

Corrections applied to v1 of this document:

1. **Ash Lord's death (major):** Magnus au Grimmus was slowly poisoned starting ~750 PCE via a scheme **Apollonius au Valii-Rath orchestrated from Deepgrave** (revenge for his betrayal). Atalantia concealed his condition with holograms and ruled as **Dictator** from 750. He finally died **November 15, 753** at the **Battle of Gorgon Isle** on Venus, confronted by Darrow, Sevro, and Apollonius. v1's implication that the Ash Armada simply passed from Magnus to Atalantia "after the fall of Luna" understated this.
2. **Liberation of Earth dates:** the campaign ran **late 746 – early 747**, ending with the **Wolf Rain** in the first months of 747 (not "through 748"). Mop-up continued: House Grimmus driven from the Americas by 748; South Pacifica holdouts persisted to at least September 753.
3. **"Zero Legion" naming:** the Sovereign-protecting secret legion is **Legio Cohors Nihil**. Atlas au Raa commands its independent black-ops branch, **Legio Zero Pavor Nocturnus — the Gorgons** ("Night Terrors"). v1 conflated the two.
4. **Drachenjäger armament wording:** the elbow-mounted cannons are specifically **particle cannons**; the nukes are canonically just "nuclear warheads" — the term **"alpha-omega nukes" is unverified** (an *omega*-atomic is attested at the Ladon).
5. **Ship classes:** "frigate" exists in canon (10 captured at the Battle of Phobos) but is **not a documented class rung** with sizes; **moonBreakers are 6–8 km** as a class (Morning Star 8 km, Dustmaker 6 km — the only two known); **"stormWing" does not exist** (thunderWing is the bomber variant of the ripWing); Wasps are "similar to a ripWing," not canonically smaller.
6. **One hull, three names:** *Colossus* (Roque's Sword Armada flagship) → captured at Ilium and renamed ***Morning Star*** → captured by the Society Remnant in Feb 754 and rebuilt by Lysander as ***Lightbringer***. Never treat these as separate ships.
7. **Propulsion terms:** "ion engines," "inertial dampeners," and "blackmatter" have **no wiki support** — treat as book-text-only/low-confidence. Canon-attested: helium-3 as the Society's primary energy source, nuclear reactors aboard ships (Morning Star "multiple nuclear reactors"; Archimedes six reactors), named engine models (Hermes-30 "coldwhisper"), gravity generators. No FTL (verified by implication: Mars→Belt in under 4 weeks is *fast*).
8. **Storm Gods:** regulate weather on worlds **being** terraformed, then are officially decommissioned (by the 8th century PCE only Triton and Pluto were believed to still have them). Tyche was destroyed by a **kilometer-high tidal wave**, not sandstorms (the sandstorm devastated the Society landing waves in the Waste of Ladon). Darrow killed Orion via a **pre-installed failsafe built by Glirastes**.
9. **"The Ladon" is the Waste of Ladon — a desert**, not a waterway.
10. **Rat War:** wiki gives both 748 and 749 start dates (internal inconsistency). Gorgon atrocities are documented chiefly from the Mercury campaign (753–754), not itemized for the Rat War.
11. **Mercury Iron Rain date:** the wiki is internally inconsistent — **July 753** (Timeline, Siege of Mercury page; better supported) vs August 753 (Iron Rain, Free Legions pages). It occurs at the **end of *Iron Gold***, on the continent of **Borealis**.
12. **Eleventh Legion ("Golden Basilisks"):** cornered against a dam with no avenue of retreat by a legion under Darrow's personal command; all 50,000 fought to the death — Republic troops fired into the formation continuously for 8 hours (*Iron Gold* ch. 51). (Battle of Luna infobox mis-attributes this to an "8th Legion"; body text and Legion page support the Eleventh.)
13. **"Burner" as a sidearm:** unverified; burner = cigarette is solidly attested. Dropped the sidearm claim to a footnote.
14. **Ceres:** trading post + one of the Society's three primary helium-3 stockpiles (the "War Reserve") is canon; "naval base" is not attested; the ~940 km diameter is the real-world figure, not book canon.
15. **Home Guard vs Ecliptic Guard:** Home Guard defends **Luna and Earth**; the Ecliptic Guard (4th Fleet + Belt defense fleets) defends **Mars** and guards the Belt approaches (it fought defending Phobos in *Light Bringer*).

Unverifiable-but-plausible items retained and labeled: burner-as-sidearm, alpha-omega naming, frigate's ladder position, ion-engine/inertial-dampener/blackmatter propulsion details.

# 1. How large-scale warfare works

A typical fleet engagement has several overlapping layers:

1. **Long range:** missiles and torpedoes.
2. **Medium range:** railgun barrages (at Ilium, railgun exchange opened at roughly **300 km**, with a **50 km "no-man's-land"** between battle lines).
3. **Defensive envelope:** flak, point-defense guns, electronic countermeasures, and pulseShields.
4. **Closer engagement:** particle-cannon broadsides, fighters, bombers, and ship-to-ship maneuver.
5. **Boarding:** leechCraft (max 50 soldiers, thermal drill + shield), clawDrills, and starShell troops.
6. **Planetary operations:** orbital bombardment, artillery, atomics, mechanized armor, and Iron Rains.

The logic is combined arms:

- Missiles pressure targets from long range.
- Flak and point defense protect formations.
- Railguns batter shields and punch through hulls.
- Particle cannons deliver destructive line-of-sight energy fire.
- Fighters attack exposed systems and defend capital ships.
- Boarding forces exploit disabled or damaged ships.
- Legions seize and hold the surface after the fleet establishes orbital control.

No single weapon dominates all ranges.

### Iron Rain (verified definition)

A mass orbital invasion: Golds and Obsidians in starShell armor fired from **spitTubes**; Grays follow in **hivepods of twelve**. The Lion's Rain (742) was the first Iron Rain in 20 years. Darrow's Mercury Rain (753) cost 1 million Republic dead in a single day; Atalantia's "Ash Rain" (Jan 754) committed ~30 million warColor soldiers.

# 2. Strategic and fleet weapons

## 2.1 Railguns

Railguns electromagnetically accelerate solid projectiles to extreme velocity.

They appear at many scales:

- Capital-ship batteries (the dreadnought *Pax* mounts **80+ railguns**)
- Surface and fortress batteries (e.g., the Twins of South Pacifica installation that contested the Fall of Earth)
- Vehicle-mounted guns
- Fighter guns
- Infantry railRifles
- Rare handheld systems such as the Omnivore-540

### Primary effects

Railguns are mainly **kinetic penetration weapons**:

- Punch through hull plating
- Overload pulseShields through repeated impacts
- Destroy armored vehicles and starShells
- Create lethal internal fragmentation after hull penetration
- Conduct orbital-to-surface bombardment

A railgun projectile does not need a large explosive warhead if its kinetic energy is already enormous.

### RailRifle

The infantry-scale version. Concentrated fire can overload pulseShields, dent armor, and eventually penetrate or crush the target underneath. (A railgun round killed Seraphina au Raa at the Ladon.)

### Omnivore-540 (verified)

A rare semiautomatic handheld railgun made by **Titan Arms** — only **~20,000 produced** in a limited run (early-to-mid 690s PCE). Powered by rechargeable ion cells driving rounds along parallel reactive conductors; adjustable internal bore (multi-caliber); an autonomous forge inside the magazine manufactures ammunition from any metal inserted. Ephraim ti Horn carries one.

### Scorcher

A lighter electromagnetic projectile weapon. Easier to carry and more common than a railRifle, but generally less effective against elite pulseArmor without concentrated fire.

---

## 2.2 Particle cannons and particle beams

Particle cannons are directed-energy weapons.

They are portrayed as producing:

- Extreme heat
- Burning and ablation
- Vaporization
- Catastrophic structural damage
- Near-immediate line-of-sight strikes

They are used as:

- Capital-ship main batteries
- Orbital-strike weapons
- Surface anti-aircraft guns
- Fortress weapons (Gorgon Isle's citadel mounted particle cannons and railguns)
- Drachenjäger elbow cannons (canon-specific)
- Frigate or dropship main guns

### Battlefield role

Particle cannons are effective for:

- Vaporizing aircraft
- Destroying gun positions
- Burning through ships and fortifications
- Destroying massed starShell formations
- Conducting precision orbital strikes
- Superheating enclosed habitats or atmospheric zones

The exact fictional physics are not fully explained.

---

## 2.3 Missiles and torpedoes

Missiles and torpedoes are guided weapons used by:

- Capital ships
- RipWings
- ThunderWings
- StarShells
- Drachenjägers
- Infantry teams
- Surface batteries
- Hidden planetary launch sites (the Ascomanni fielded thousands of torpedo launchers at the Kalyke Massacre)

### Major types

- Conventional anti-ship missiles
- Anti-fighter missiles
- Anti-ground missiles
- Torpedoes
- Bunkerbusters
- PulseRockets
- Nuclear missiles and torpedoes
- Sarissa launchers
- Baritone rockets

### Torpedoes

Usually larger guided munitions associated with ship-killing, underwater operations, or surface-to-orbit ambushes.

### Bunkerbusters

Heavy penetration bombs designed to break hardened structures before detonating.

### Sarissa missile launcher

A large shoulder-fired anti-aircraft or anti-shuttle missile launcher.

### Baritone

A Rim rocket launcher designed to penetrate fortifications.

### PulseRocket

A compact pulse-warhead rocket powerful enough to kill vehicles or shoot down smaller ships.

---

## 2.4 Flak

Flak is the main weapon system that produces broad fragment clouds to destroy ripWings, leechCraft, boarding craft, and incoming munitions.

### How it works

Flak shells or canisters are fired toward an area and detonate at a calculated distance, creating:

- High-velocity fragments
- Clouds of pellets or debris
- Explosive pressure
- Thermal effects
- A defensive curtain small craft must cross

This allows a capital ship to threaten many agile targets without precisely hitting each one.

### Flak versus point defense

- **Flak:** fills an area with fragments and explosions.
- **Point defense:** directly tracks and fires at individual missiles, fighters, or boarding craft.
- **PulseShield:** an energy barrier protecting the ship itself.

### Flak shield

A "flak shield" is not an energy shield. It is a protective field of detonating defensive munitions around a fleet formation.

---

## 2.5 MicroShot

MicroShot is an infantry cluster munition.

One round splits into approximately thirty miniature explosive submunitions. It resembles a swarm of tiny bombs.

It is related in concept to flak but is not the standard fleet-scale anti-ripWing system.

---

## 2.6 Atomics

"Atomics" is the series' common term for nuclear weapons.

They appear as:

- Ship-launched nuclear missiles
- Tactical battlefield nukes (an **omega-atomic** vaporized the Red Reach base and 1 million troops of the Republic Second Army at the Ladon)
- Drachenjäger-carried warheads
- Strategic stockpiles (Octavia stockpiled **500 warheads** in a secret asteroid-belt depot after Rhea; the Jackal stole 500 × 30-megaton nukes and planted them across Luna before The Fall)
- Planetary or lunar annihilation weapons

### Strategic role

Atomics can:

- Destroy concentrated armor
- Wipe out cities (New Thebes, destroyed from orbit by the Ash Lord ~747–749)
- Break fortified regions
- Irradiate battlefields
- Destroy fleets
- Terrorize political populations

### The Burning of Rhea (verified, dated)

**August 701 PCE.** Rhea — Saturn's second-largest moon, population ~60 million, leading world of the **First Moon Lord Rebellion** — was destroyed by nuclear orbital bombardment, carried out by ArchImperator Magnus au Grimmus on Sovereign Octavia au Lune's direct order, *during* the rebellion, to terrify the other Moon Lords into capitulating (it worked). It earned Magnus the name "Ash Lord," ruptured his friendship with Lorn au Arcos, and is memorialized in the Rim ballad "A Memory of Ash." The defining example of strategic nuclear annihilation.

## 2.7 Orbital bombardment

Orbital bombardment is a method, not one specific weapon.

It can employ:

- Particle beams
- Railgun projectiles
- Missiles
- Atomics
- Conventional explosive bombardment

Ground troops and starShell operators often designate targets for orbiting ships (starShells carry targeting lasers for exactly this).

## 2.9 EMP weapons

Electromagnetic-pulse weapons disable electronics rather than directly destroying targets.

They can shut down:

- GravBoots
- StarShells
- PulseArmor
- Vehicles
- Drones
- Communications
- Targeting equipment
- Powered weapons

(An EMP was the Free Legions' planned escape mechanism from Heliopolis; Glirastes' sabotage of it doomed them during the Long Night.)

### Countermeasures

EMP-resistant or non-powered equipment includes:

- Razors
- Ambi-Rifles (gas-powered)
- Slug shooters
- ScarabSkin and related armor (not battery-powered)

## 2.10 Gravity weapons

### GravWell

Creates an artificial gravity field capable of:

- Pulling down aircraft or ships
- Pinning people
- Trapping rooms
- Redirecting movement

### Gravity gun

A heavy mechanized gravity weapon — one of the Society Titan's three main cannons. At the Battle of the Ladon, Scorpio au Votum's Titan used its gravity gun to halt two charging Drachenjägers.

### GravFist

A gauntlet that manipulates local gravity to:

- Repel targets
- Suspend debris
- Stop or redirect projectiles
- Reverse incoming munitions

### GravRifle

Produces a powerful concussive gravity blast capable of throwing a person a significant distance.

## 2.11 Storm Gods as weapons

Storm Gods are terraforming machines converted into strategic environmental weapons. (Canonically terraforming machines *used* as weapons — not purpose-built war machines.)

They can manipulate:

- Atmospheric pressure
- Wind systems
- Rainfall
- Oceanic weather
- Electrical storms
- Hypercanes
- Sandstorms

They can devastate armies and cities over enormous areas.

A fuller explanation appears in [Storm Gods and Lovelock engines](#15-storm-gods-and-lovelock-engines).

## 2.12 Mechanized war machines

### StarShell (verified)

A **four-meter-tall, ~three-ton** mechanized full-body combat shell — "mobile tanks."

Common weapons include:

- PulseCannons
- Missile launchers
- Razors or heavy melee weapons
- Targeting lasers for orbital strikes
- Modular, field-customizable weapon systems

Flight via gravBoots plus shoulder thrusters; launched en masse from spitTubes in Iron Rains. Withstands most weapons short of large railguns and razors.

### Drachenjäger (verified)

A **forty-meter-tall Republic** war machine (developed 744–747 PCE) with:

- Six separate arms
- Jointed articulation
- **Particle cannons mounted on the elbows**
- Huge ion cleavers
- An arsenal of nuclear warheads
- Heavy armor
- Headless, boxy, hunched silhouette with a spiked backpack; cockpit between the shoulders
- Multi-person or linked control system

The Armored 15th Legion fielded ~5,000 Drachenjäger pilots at Agea; the Republic had **≥10,000 Drachenjägers** on Mercury.

### Titan (verified)

A **sixty-meter-tall Society Remnant** war machine, built during the Solar War specifically to counter Drachenjägers, with:

- Four legs
- Three arms
- **Three main cannons — one of which is a massive gravity gun**
- Disk-shaped cockpit
- Heavy armor

## 2.13 Fighters, bombers, and boarding craft

### RipWing (verified)

The primary fighter craft: **13 meters**, single pilot (Blues, or Gold squadron leaders), operating in **squadrons of 12**.

Weapons include:

- Chainguns / rapid-fire guns
- Railguns
- Missiles
- Bombs on some variants

Roles include:

- Fighter interception
- Strafing
- Ground attack
- Escort

### Wasp

A military space fighter similar to a ripWing, used in screening and interception. (Not canonically smaller.)

### ThunderWing

A heavier, spider-shaped ripWing variant used for bombing runs and bunkerbuster attacks. (Note: "stormWing" is not a canon craft — only Storm-class *corvettes* exist.)

### LeechCraft (verified)

Boarding craft that cross a fleet's defensive envelope, attach to enemy hulls, and deliver troops — **maximum 50 soldiers**, equipped with a thermal drill and shield. Complements: destroyers carry ~4; dreadnoughts up to ~200.

### Other small craft (enrichment)

- **Storks** — troop transports, larger than leechCraft
- **Hivepods** — carry 12 Grays in an Iron Rain
- **Pelican** dropships and **RTA-16** troop carriers

### ClawDrill

An industrial mining machine converted into a giant boarding weapon capable of drilling through a capital ship's hull. See [Section 9](#9-clawdrills).

## 3.4 Razors (verified)

The signature weapon of Golds — forbidden to other Colors on pain of death. Built by Orange "Bladeweavers."

A razor's blade is made of **polyenne fiber** (solid form harder than diamond) and toggles via a hilt button between:

- A rigid sword (~1 meter in blade form)
- A flexible whip roughly twice the blade's length
- Other selected shapes

Advantages:

- Cuts through advanced armor (pierces recoilArmor and pulseArmor)
- Resists EMPs
- Changes geometry during combat
- Excels in boarding and close quarters

Razors remain effective in a high-tech setting because elite combatants can close distance quickly, shields complicate ranged attacks, and razors can penetrate defenses that stop ordinary weapons.

## 3.10 Chemical, biological, and psychological weapons

### Achlys-9 (verified)

A deadly poison gas used for executions and in mine "liquidation protocol" quarantines by the Board of Quality Control.

### Heliopolis chemical weapon

A chemical agent designed to kill living things while leaving buildings and infrastructure intact. (Atalantia's planned chemical extermination of Heliopolis is what pushed Atlas to smuggle Lysander into the city before the Long Night.)

### Ceronocyne

A sleeping gas that can be tuned to affect selected Colors.

### Solocene

A sterilization gas used after the Conquering.

### Eidmi (verified)

A bioweapon virus with a modular half-life capable of targeting any one of the fourteen Colors (including Gold) without secondary transmission. Developed by Silenius au Lune (early 1st century PCE); stolen by Akari au Raa.

Strategic effect:

- Color-specific genocide
- Infrastructure remains intact
- Territory can be occupied afterward
- Other Colors may survive

### Pandemonium Chair (verified)

A machine that records, sifts, edits, and erases memories — stimulating neuron activity in the prefrontal cortex and temporal lobes and converting it into storable audiovisual replications; edits alter the genuine memory. Built by Violet Carvers and Orange Master Makers on Octavia au Lune's order (before 738 PCE). Used on Lysander to erase memories of his mother.

### PsychoSpike

A miniaturized memory and identity weapon (paired with the Chair for deep neural hacking) capable of:

- Extracting memories
- Deleting memories
- Destroying identity
- Paralyzing the body
- Disabling language
- Manipulating emotional reward pathways

# 4. Skyhooks

A skyhook is a large elevated platform, port, airbase, or complex suspended by gravity technology.

It is not necessarily the real-world theoretical rotating orbital tether.

## Uses

### Civilian spaceport

A floating launch and docking facility positioned several kilometers above a city.

Functions may include:

- Runways
- Yacht docks
- Passenger lounges
- Reception areas
- Cargo transfer
- Interplanetary departure facilities

### Private structure

A skyhook can also be a private office, headquarters, or estate.

### Military base

A military skyhook can serve as:

- Fighter base
- Gunboat base
- StarShell staging area
- Drachenjäger garage
- Ammunition depot
- Command center
- Communications hub
- Transfer point between surface and orbit

Best comparison:

> A skyhook is a floating combination of aircraft carrier, airbase, spaceport, and logistics platform.

# 5. Capital ships and ship classes

"Capital ship" is a category, not one exact model.

Capital ships are large warships important enough to form the core of a fleet.

Their functions can include:

- Missile and torpedo warfare
- Railgun barrages
- Particle-cannon fire
- Flak and point defense
- Fighter deployment
- Boarding operations
- Iron Rain deployment
- Fleet command
- Troop and equipment transport

## 5.1 Corvette (verified)

A small warship (50 m to a few hundred meters; crew up to 30, up to 40 infantry; larger ones carry ~4 leechCraft) used for:

- Scouting
- Patrol
- Raiding
- Escort
- Independent missions

Known sub-classes: **Whisper** (50+ m, 3 guns), **Storm** (8 guns), **Thunder** (civilian), **Hermes** (100+ m).

## 5.2 Frigate (canon term, undocumented class)

Frigates exist (10 were among the ships captured at the Battle of Phobos) but canon gives no sizes or class detail. Their placement between corvette and torchShip is inference.

## 5.3 TorchShip (verified)

**500–800 m; crew ~600; up to 5,000 infantry** (military models). A large interplanetary transport type; "torchShip" describes propulsion and transport function more than one uniform combat class. TorchShip engines have nuclear cores that can detonate catastrophically.

## 5.4 Destroyer (verified)

A common mid-level fleet warship, **~1.5 km** average; carries ~4 leechCraft; costs roughly **47 billion credits** (*Dark Age* ch. 25).

Typical roles:

- Escort
- Screening
- Hunting damaged ships
- Supporting battle lines
- Adding missile and railgun volume

## 5.5 Dreadnought or man-of-war (verified)

A heavy capital ship **4–6 km** long; crew **20,000–80,000**; up to **500,000 infantry**; up to 4 hangar bays and **~200 leechCraft**.

Roles include:

- Fleet flagship
- Battle-line anchor
- Heavy bombardment platform
- Fighter carrier
- Boarding platform
- Iron Rain deployment ship
- Mobile command center

## 5.6 MoonBreaker (verified)

The super-capital tier: **6–8 km long, up to 1.5 km tall** — the largest and rarest class. Only two are known: the ***Morning Star*** (8 km) and the ***Dustmaker*** (6 km).

A MoonBreaker functions as:

- Super-dreadnought
- Massive carrier
- Mobile invasion base
- Strategic command center
- Planetary-bombardment platform

## 5.7 Flagship

A flagship is not a class.

It is the ship carrying the fleet commander and command staff.

# 7. Capital-ship propulsion

Brown explains the components and behavior but not a complete engineering system.

**Canon-attested:** helium-3 is the Society's primary energy source, "powering spacecraft, infrastructure and terraforming"; ships carry nuclear reactors (the *Morning Star* is "powered by multiple nuclear reactors"; the *Archimedes* has six); named engine models exist (Hermes-30 "coldwhisper"); artificial gravity comes from gravity generators.

**Book-text-only / low confidence (no wiki support):** "ion engines," "inertial dampeners," and "blackmatter." Treat these as unverified flavor rather than established canon.

The best working model:

1. Helium-3-fueled reactors generate enormous power.
2. High-thrust engines produce propulsion.
3. Rear engines provide main acceleration; lateral thrusters handle turning and translation.
4. Ships remain slower than light and require meaningful travel time.

## 7.1 No faster-than-light travel

The series remains confined to the Solar System. Mars→Belt in under 4 weeks is considered *fast* (post-upgrade Archimedes). Interplanetary trips take weeks to months and significant logistical planning.

# 8. Armor and shields

A useful layered model is:

1. Physical armor
2. Energy shielding
3. Powered systems
4. Mechanized shell

## 8.1 DuroArmor

Basic military armor.

Protects against:

- Ordinary blades
- Some low-powered projectiles
- Debris
- Glancing impacts

Weak against:

- Razors
- Heavy rail weapons
- High-energy pulse or ion weapons

## 8.2 Durosteel armor

Physical armor made from advanced metal.

Stronger than ordinary steel but still conventional and vulnerable to razors, ion weapons, and heavy energy fire.

## 8.3 ScarabSkin (verified)

Light stealth armor.

Characteristics:

- Skin-tight
- Absorbs light (renders the wearer pitch-black)
- No thermal signature
- Flexible
- Not battery-powered — EMP-immune
- Slightly more durable than duroArmor

It is optimized for infiltration, mobility, and special operations.

### Variants (verified)

- **LizardSkin:** lighter/thinner, desert operations
- **SealSkin:** polar temperatures and water immersion

## 8.4 RecoilArmor

Advanced heavy physical armor.

Functions include:

- Absorbing projectile impact
- Distributing force
- Resisting railRifle hits
- Closing around penetrations
- Compressing wounds
- Administering medical support

### RecoilPlate

Usually refers to heavy plate components or armor worn beneath a larger shell such as a starShell.

## 8.5 PulseArmor

Elite powered battle armor.

Combines:

- Heavy physical armor
- PulseShield
- Sensors
- Communications
- Medical support
- Environmental sealing
- GravBoot compatibility
- Powered movement
- Weapon interfaces

Weaknesses:

- Shield overload
- Heat
- Power limits
- EMP disruption
- Razors
- Concentrated heavy fire

## 8.6 Godkiller armor (verified)

A late-generation pulseArmor system developed by **Regulus ag Sun (Quicksilver)**; debuts in *Light Bringer*. Light, fast, and stealthy without sacrificing power.

Three power modes:

- **Reptile:** runs cold — stealth
- **Lupine:** regular combat and travel
- **Titan:** maximum output ("to kill gods")

## 8.7 StarShell

A starShell is not just armor. It is a mechanized combat suit approximately four meters tall and weighing ~three tons. See [Section 2.12](#212-mechanized-war-machines).

During an Iron Rain, it can receive an ablative exterior layer.

### Threats capable of defeating starShells

- Heavy railguns
- Uranium rifles
- Particle weapons
- Other starShells
- Drachenjägers
- Titans
- Razors at close range
- EMPs
- Specialized armor-piercing ammunition

## 8.8 Shields

### PulseShield

A wearable energy barrier.

Can fail through:

- Repeated fire
- Heat
- Overload
- EMP disruption

### Aegis

A small buckler-like energy shield.

### PulseBubble

A large force field used around structures, estates, settlements, or habitats. (City shields matter operationally: Atlas collapsed Mercury's northern shield chain by overloading hard-lined city reactors before the Ash Rain.)

### GhostCloak

Camouflage technology, not armor.

## 8.9 Simplified protection hierarchy

From least to greatest direct battlefield protection:

**Ordinary clothing → environmental suit → duroArmor → scarabSkin → recoilArmor → pulseArmor → Godkiller armor → starShell**

This is not absolute. ScarabSkin may be better for infiltration, and SealSkin may be better underwater.

# 9. ClawDrills

A clawDrill is a giant industrial mining machine shaped metaphorically like a titanic metal hand. Canon length: **90 meters**; normally mines helium-3, operated by Red Helldivers.

## 9.1 Physical arrangement

The operator sits in a holster seat near the "elbow" area, isolated from the support crew.

Approximately ninety meters below the operator are multiple tentacle-like drilling fingers.

That means:

- The ninety-meter figure is the distance from operator to drilling appendages
- The entire machine may be slightly longer
- It is skyscraper-length but narrow and tunnel-oriented

## 9.2 What a HellDiver is doing

A HellDiver does not ride astride a drill bit.

The operator:

- Sits near the upper or rear structure
- Inserts hands into control gloves
- Controls multiple heat-lit drilling digits
- Coordinates fine finger movements
- Descends while the machine melts and chews through rock

The support crew remains in the tunnel above.

## 9.3 How it works

The clawDrill uses:

- Multiple independently controlled fingers
- Superheated or molten cutting surfaces
- Replaceable or cycled drill bits
- A long arm-like structure
- Enormous vibration and heat

It produces a recognizable gnawing and grinding sound underground.

## 9.4 Weaponized version (verified at the Battle of Ilium)

During ship boarding, mining clawDrills are modified with:

- Engines
- Armor
- Reinforced cockpit
- Space-launch capability
- Superheated tungsten-carbide teeth

At Ilium, **four clawDrills hidden aboard the *Pax*** (alongside 30 heavy assault shuttles and 3,000 Obsidians) drilled out through the *Pax*'s own decks to breach Roque's *Colossus* — Roque's gunBlues couldn't target them because the vessel classification didn't register in their targeting systems. Assault shuttles then entered the tunnels they created.

# 11. Solar War chronology between *Morning Star* and *Iron Gold*

The strategic progression is:

**Luna → Earth → Mars → Ceres and the asteroid belt → Mercury**

The Republic wins most major territorial campaigns, but at catastrophic cost.

## 11.1 Battle of Luna and the Block Wars — January 744 – late 746 PCE

- The Solar War begins January 744: the Ash Lord returns with his newly assembled Ash Armada to retake Luna.
- Urban fighting devastates whole blocks over three years ("the Block Wars").
- The **Eleventh Legion (Golden Basilisks)** is cornered against a dam with no avenue of retreat by a legion under Darrow's personal command; all 50,000 fight to the death — Republic troops fire into the formation continuously for 8 hours (*Iron Gold* ch. 51).
- The Society withdraws late 746.

**Result:** Republic holds Luna.

## 11.2 Liberation of Earth — late 746 – early 747 (mop-up to 748+)

Major episodes include:

- Invasion of Grimmus-held northern Africa (September–November 746); House Grimmus driven from its ancestral seat **New Sparta**
- Darrow and the 8th Legion vs Apollonius at **Khardung La**
- Fighting at the City Gates of **Paris**
- **Battle of Gibraltar** — Darrow used a Drachenjäger charge to disguise an ambush
- Skirmishes over the Bay of Bengal (Thraxa saves Valdir)
- The **Wolf Rain** (first months of 747) — the Iron Rain that drove off the Society fleet and officially liberated the planet

**Result:** Republic captures nearly all Earth by early 747. Last Grimmus forces driven from the Americas by 748; South Pacifica holdouts persist to at least September 753.

## 11.3 Siege of Olympia — early 747

- Apollonius au Valii-Rath and Atlas au Raa seize Olympia (former Bellona seat) on Mars.
- Multi-week Republic siege, largely by Obsidians under Sefi and Valdir (Valdir survives a duel with Apollonius).
- Darrow arrives with the 7th and 15th Legions and drives to Eagle Rest; Apollonius and Atlas retreat.
- Olympia is left in ruins; later given to the Obsidian diaspora.

**Result:** Republic captures Olympia.

## 11.4 Battle of Zephyria — 747

A major early Martian engagement with limited surviving detail; the Society fielded a unit of **1,000+ Peerless Scarred**.

**Result:** Part of the Republic's expanding control of Mars.

## 11.5 Capture of Apollonius — 747

The Ash Lord sends Apollonius, 100 of his best troops, and 900 Carthii Golds on a mission to assassinate Darrow and Virginia's family on Luna — a **deliberate suicide mission**, so the Ash Lord and House Carthii could pillage House Valii-Rath's fleet and legions. Apollonius is captured and sentenced to life in Deepgrave.

**Result:** Society loses a major field commander — and creates the enemy who will poison the Ash Lord (750) from inside Deepgrave.

## 11.6 Battles for Agea and New Thebes — late 747 – early 749

- **Battle of Agea:** Darrow rides with the Armored 15th Legion (~5,000 Drachenjäger pilots) against the legions of Magnus and Atlas; the Martian capital is liberated.
- **Battle of New Thebes:** after the Republic takes the city, the Ash Lord **destroys it by nuclear orbital bombardment**; the annihilation drives several minor Martian Gold houses to the Republic.

**Result:** Strategic Republic advance with catastrophic civilian destruction.

## 11.7 Battle of Mars — early 749

A major fleet battle in Martian orbit — one of the largest Society materiel expenditures of the war, rivaled only by the Ladon.

**Result:** Society fleet expelled from Martian orbit. (Surface declared free 751; Sirenia holdouts to late 753.)

## 11.8 Rat War — 748/749 – 751

A prolonged underground campaign to liberate Mars's helium-3 mines. (Wiki gives both 748 and 749 start dates.)

Characteristics:

- Tunnel fighting
- Ambushes
- Booby traps
- Night raids
- Infiltration
- Psychological warfare
- Considered worse than the Block Wars; mining townships left as "empty necropolises"

Major Republic leaders: **Darrow, Sevro, Dancer O'Faran, Sefi Volarus, Valdir** (Dancer personally liberated Lagalos in 751). Society commander: **Atlas au Raa**, with Legio Zero Pavor Nocturnus (Gorgons). (The Gorgons' itemized atrocities are documented chiefly from the Mercury campaign; their Rat War brutality is implied.)

**Result:** Republic liberates the mines and remaining major Society territories on Mars.

## 11.9 Battle of Ceres — ~750 (wiki estimate; "prior to 751")

- Darrow and Orion xe Aquarii lead the White Fleet.
- The *Morning Star* nearly breaks the *Annihilo* (Magnus commanding) in half; the *Annihilo* destroys two Republic destroyers and holds until Carthii reinforcements arrive.
- Total Society withdrawal.

**Result:** Republic captures Ceres.

## 11.10 Siege of Mercury — 751 to July 753

- Republic besieges Mercury for roughly two years (White Fleet blockade; ≥400 warships; 10–11M legionnaires; ≥10,000 Drachenjägers — against ~30M Society warColor soldiers).
- The Senate (Dancer O'Faran, Publius cu Caraval) refuses to authorize an Iron Rain; Atalantia uses delay and political warfare (including the false peace embassy of Julia au Bellona and Asmodeus au Carthii).
- Glirastes the Master Maker defects (July 753) in exchange for Darrow's promise to spare population centers.

## 11.11 Mercury Iron Rain — July 753 (some wiki pages: August)

- Defying the Senate, Darrow launches the Iron Rain on the continent of **Borealis** at the end of *Iron Gold*.
- Approximately **1 million Republic soldiers die in a single day** (including 200,000 Obsidians).
- The Ash Armada is driven off; Mercury falls.
- **Atlas au Raa and the Gorgons remain hidden** on the surface, harrying Republic forces.
- Political consequences: Darrow stripped of ArchImperator rank with an arrest warrant; Sefi withdraws nearly all Obsidians to Mars (the Alltribe); command passes to Harnassus; **half the White Fleet is recalled to Luna** — the direct setup for the Battle of Caliban disaster.

**Result:** Republic captures Mercury — and fatally overextends.

## 11.12 Did the Republic basically win every battle?

At the strategic level, mostly yes.

By *Iron Gold*, the Republic controls:

- Luna
- Earth
- Mars
- Phobos
- Deimos
- Much of the Belt
- Ceres
- Mercury

But the victories are deceptive.

### Why the Republic is still in danger

- It often drives enemy fleets away instead of destroying them.
- It suffers enormous casualties.
- It overextends supply lines.
- It disperses forces across hostile worlds.
- The Senate and military leadership fracture politically.
- Atlas attacks through infiltration and terror.
- The Society preserves veteran commanders and major fleets.
- The Republic reaches maximum territorial extent while becoming institutionally brittle.

# 15. Storm Gods and Lovelock engines

These are complementary terraforming systems.

| Machine | Controls | Primary purpose |
|---|---|---|
| Lovelock engine | Crust, mantle, tectonics | Shapes and stabilizes the physical world |
| Storm God | Atmosphere, weather, oceanic circulation | Builds and regulates climate during terraforming |

---

## 15.1 Lovelock engines (verified)

A Lovelock engine is a planetary-scale tectonic control system — "the machine Golds use to manipulate the tectonic plates of a planet."

### Physical arrangement

Each planet or moon undergoing terraforming is equipped with **two engines, one at each pole**. They tower dozens of stories above the surface, each burrowing down and described as meeting its counterpart near the core.

This may mean:

- Literal machinery
- Energy conduits
- Gravity fields
- Deep shafts acting together

Brown does not explain which.

### Capabilities

Lovelock engines can:

- Move or redirect tectonic plates
- Raise mountain ranges (they created the Valkyrie Spires on Mars's south pole)
- Create valleys
- Shape plains
- Stabilize earthquakes
- Control volcanic activity
- Reshape terrain over decades

They are not instant mountain generators. They apply massive forces over long periods.

### Likely mechanism (inference)

The most plausible model combines:

- Gravity manipulation
- Controlled mantle heating or cooling
- Deep mechanical or energy conduits
- Artificial stress on tectonic plates

### Why they need huge workforces

Canon: **30,000 workers to run one**. A Lovelock is an industrial megaproject requiring reactor operation, structural maintenance, cooling, deep-bore repair, geological monitoring, earthquake prediction, evacuation planning, and continuous control. The machine can also be destroyed by the geology it manipulates.

## 15.2 Storm Gods (verified)

A Storm God is a mobile atmospheric terraforming platform whose top hull is roughly **1 kilometer in diameter**, with **gravity engines underneath** enabling flight.

Visual description (canon): an upside-down turtle shell pierced by a trident.

### Original purpose

Giant weather-shaping machines used **alongside Lovelock engines while a world is being terraformed** — regulating atmospheric pressure, wind, rainfall, moisture distribution, electrical storms, oceanic weather, and climate patterns. Officially decommissioned once terraforming completes; by the 8th century PCE only Triton and Pluto were believed to still have active ones.

### Likely mechanism (inference)

Storm Gods appear to manipulate effective gravity, atmospheric pressure, heat, moisture, and air-column movement. By changing gravity or pressure, they force air to rise or sink, creating pressure gradients; air races between the gradients and forms cyclones, hypercanes, sandstorms, and massive electrical systems. They may also heat air or ocean, condense or vaporize water, ionize clouds, seed lightning, and steer storms by shifting their field.

### Why storms continue after shutdown

A Storm God does not need to provide all the storm's energy continuously. It initiates a circulation pattern that becomes self-sustaining, feeding on warm ocean water, moisture, pressure differences, and momentum. That is why the storms persisted after Darrow shut the system down.

### Mercury deployment (verified)

**Seven Storm Gods** were covertly buried by House Lune beneath Mercury's polar ice caps as a contingency against rebellion by the ruling Votum family, with the records kept in the Crescent Vault on Luna. Darrow learned of them after Virginia's hackers opened the vault (747).

In **January 754** the Free Legions excavated and reactivated them — **Operation Tartarus** — with the reluctant help of Glirastes, moving them to the Sycorax Sea, crewed by Orion and her Blues. Darrow's intended goals:

- Scramble Society comms, sensors, and electronics
- Ground enemy aircraft
- Break Society landing formations
- Conceal Republic movements and protect Republic forces

Darrow promised Glirastes they would run at the lowest setting; Glirastes built a **kill-switch failsafe** into the system as insurance.

### Orion's escalation (verified, clarified)

Orion — traumatized by three weeks of Gorgon torture — took the Storm Gods **above the primary horizon** in defiance of Darrow's orders, aiming to punish the people of Mercury, and attempted to power them to **"Level Four" / the fourth horizon**, which would have scoured the entire surface of the planet.

Effects:

- **Five hypercanes** over the Sycorax Sea plus a giant sandstorm over the Waste of Ladon
- The sandstorm annihilated the Society's first and second landing waves (60 transports destroyed in the first minute)
- A **kilometer-high tidal wave** drowned a third of **Tyche** and buckled its oceanfront (the drowning Ash Legions that had breached the city died with it; only the Water Colossus stood intact)
- Several cities destroyed; millions killed

Darrow killed Orion **via Glirastes' pre-installed failsafe**, forcibly disconnecting her to avert planetary destruction. The storms raged on.

### Primary horizon and Level Four

The "primary horizon" is the safe/controlled operating envelope; "Level Four" (the fourth horizon) is an extreme output capable of scouring a planet's surface. Both terms are canon-attested.

### Vulnerability

Storm Gods depend on enormous gravity-engine arrays. Destroying the gravity system removes lift, disrupts the pressure anomaly, causes the machine to fall, and weakens its active control over the storm. Lysander destroyed the desert Storm God during the Ladon (the action in which Seraphina au Raa was killed by a railgun round). The storm may continue because the atmosphere has already been accelerated.

## 15.3 How the two systems fit together

A simplified terraforming sequence:

1. **Lovelocks reshape the world** — raise mountains, form basins, stabilize crust, control tectonics
2. **Water and atmosphere are established**
3. **Storm Gods organize the climate** — move heat and moisture, generate rain, establish circulation, regulate weather
4. **Both systems stabilize the world**, then Storm Gods are decommissioned

Best analogy:

> **Lovelock engines sculpt the planet's body. Storm Gods establish its breathing and circulation.**
